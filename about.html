The content on this website is produced by me, Jesse Barber.

I was born and raised in Berkeley, California. I went to Berkeley public schools my whole life and in 12th grade, served as the Berkeley Unified School District School Board Representative. In 2015, I started at Brown University. Concentrating in Urban Studies I am passionate about United States 20th century urban history and how space and where people live influence numerous public realities – education, health, job access, etc. I am fascinated with mapping as a tool for visualizing and communicating spatial histories and realities, and thus working towards more just communities.

In 2017, I helped start the Bay Area Immigration Bond Fund, an organization that helps free undocumented individuals from ICE detention so they can get better quality representation and return to their communties and homes during deportation proceedings. In a year of operation, we have rasied over $150,000 and helped free over 20 indivduals from ICE custody.

I received a Royce Fellowship the summer 2018 to pursue an independent research project about 20th century exclusionary housing policy in Berkeley. At the conclusion of the project I wrote multiple articles about contemperary effects of resedential exclusion in Berkeleyside, Berkeley’s local media outlet.

In my freetime I enjoy playing basketball and spending time outdoors.
